Semester Reflection

What I learned: Across the six labs, I developed a stronger understanding of how digital tools can be used to analyze and communicate information. I learned how to work with datasets, create visualizations using Tableau Public, and organize my work into a professional portfolio website. I also gained experience documenting my process and explaining the reasoning behind my design and analytical decisions. These labs helped me see how data, design, and digital communication all work together.]

Challenges I overcame: One of the biggest challenges was learning new tools and figuring out how to troubleshoot problems when things didn’t work the first time. For example, preparing datasets and getting them to upload correctly sometimes required cleaning the data or adjusting the format. I worked through these challenges by experimenting with different solutions, reviewing instructions, and testing multiple approaches until the tools worked properly. Over time, this helped me become more confident in solving technical problems on my own.

How I grew: My understanding of digital culture and data analytics improved significantly throughout the semester. At the beginning, I mostly thought about data as numbers, but I learned that data can also be used to tell stories and communicate insights. I also became more aware of how digital platforms shape the way information is presented and interpreted. Through the labs, I developed a better ability to analyze data and present it in a clear and meaningful way.

Looking ahead: The skills I developed in this course will be useful in many areas of my future work, especially in marketing and digital communication. Being able to analyze data, create visualizations, and present information clearly are important skills in today’s digital environment. I also plan to continue building and improving my portfolio website so that it can showcase my work and skills as I pursue internships and professional opportunities.
